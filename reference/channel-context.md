## Performance signal — what the numbers say
*Read, not gospel. Worth testing against, not obeying.*

- **The contrarian standalone is the format that pops.** #5 (~50K) is a provocative, self-contained claim with a "DPS IS A LIE" thumbnail. It roughly doubled the next-best video (#1, ~20K) and dwarfs everything else. The bold, argue-with-me hook works.
- **The trilogy decayed hard: 50K → 4.4K → 2.5K.** Parts 2 and 3 kept a small core but shed the crowd #5 pulled in. Likely because "Moons of Peril pt.3" reads as homework for people who saw pt.1, while "DPS IS A LIE" reads as a standalone provocation to everyone. **Lesson: even when a topic is a series to you, title and frame each entry as a standalone argument** with its own hook, not "part N."
- **Titles that reframe a known thing as a lie/con/secret outperform descriptive titles.** "The Strength Bonus Propaganda", "28 Slots of Genius", "MANIPULATING YOUR DAMAGE". The winners promise to overturn something the viewer thought they knew — which is also exactly what a good thesis does (`CLAUDE.md` §3).
- **Length isn't the driver.** The 11:30 video crushed the 46:16 and 29:32 ones. Long-form is fine when the argument earns it, but length isn't buying views on its own.
- **The lore/lighthearted one-off (#3) is your lowest.** Analysis is the channel's lane; the audience shows up for arguments about design, not lore trivia.

## The exurb1a pivot — how far, how fast
The voice shift is a dial, not a switch. Your winners are already provocative and thesis-driven, which is half of the exurb1a formula. What's missing is the tonal range — the cosmic zoom-out, the tender turn, the untimely joke.

Starting default (**adjust as we test**): keep the OSRS analysis rigorous and grounded; borrow the exurb1a voice mainly at the hook, the turn, and the close — the moments where a design point opens onto something bigger about play, time, or why we grind. Don't bolt cosmic philosophy onto a straight mechanics breakdown. One or two videos can push the voice harder as an experiment; watch retention and comments.
