# Business Plan — Pivoting The Cube Analyst to a Broad Game-Design Essay Channel

*Drafted 2026-07-09. Companion docs: `channel-context.md` (current channel data), `exurb1a.md` (target register), `scripts/project-f/README.md` (the finance second-channel experiment, which this plan treats as a parallel holding, not a competitor for the same slot).*

---

## 1. Executive summary

The Cube Analyst pivots from "OSRS design analysis" to "video essays about games, in a voice people subscribe to for its own sake" — the Jacob Geller / Razbuten model, where the audience follows the writer and the games rotate. The pivot is executed **gradually, in four gated phases**, because the channel's own data says abrupt broadening fails here (the Classic WoW crossover is the catalogue's worst long-form: 8.8K views against 20K/50K for pure-OSRS essays) and the niche's data says the author-brand conversion is slow but durable (GMTK 1.69M, Geller ~1.5M, Razbuten ~900K — all built over years of consistent voice across rotating subjects).

The core mechanism: **keep the OSRS discovery funnel running while the authorial voice becomes the product**, then widen game by game only when the analytics show the author-brand out-pulling the game-brand. Every phase has entry criteria, exit criteria, and a rollback. Nothing is bet that isn't already earned.

Strategic frame: this channel is the **audience-ceiling play** (~1.5–2M realistic niche ceiling at gaming RPMs); the finance channel is the **revenue-rate play** (~5–10× ceiling at 4–8× RPM). They share a production system and a register but compete only for hours, which section 9 budgets.

---

## 2. Positioning

**One-line identity (the end state):** *essays about why games are built the way they are, and what that says about the people who can't stop playing them* — rigorous design analysis under an exurb1a register: cosmic zoom-outs, tonal whiplash, tenderness earned with real content.

**The gap being claimed.** The game-design essay niche splits into craft explainers (GMTK, Design Doc — "how it works"), literary criticism (Geller — "what it means"), and experience anthropology (Razbuten). The register this channel has been building — *design analysis that opens onto mortality, meaning, and why we grind* — sits between GMTK's rigor and Geller's feeling, and nobody owns it. The Sisyphus script is the proof-of-concept already on file: a Camus essay wearing OSRS clothes.

**What does not change in the pivot:** thesis-first construction, contrarian standalone framing (the channel's proven winner), accuracy discipline, the §2 anti-AI voice rules, the full script pipeline (outline → script → music → SEO → flags → audit).

**Name:** "The Cube Analyst" is game-agnostic enough to survive the pivot; no rebrand in phases 0–2. Revisit at phase 3 only if audience research says the name reads as Minecraft/OSRS-coded to newcomers. A rebrand is a spend of accumulated recognition — don't pay it before the recognition exists.

## 3. Market summary

*(Full workings in the two niche analyses delivered 2026-07-08/09; figures verified via Social Blade/vidIQ/Wikipedia where noted.)*

| Pond | Realistic sub ceiling | RPM | Discovery | Key risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OSRS-only (today) | ~300–900K (niche all-time best) | ~$1–5 | Captive community — cheapest cold-start on YouTube | Hard ceiling; single-game dependency |
| Broad game-design essays (this plan) | ~1.5–2M (GMTK 1.69M, Geller ~1.5M, Razbuten ~900K, Daryl ~700K) | ~$1–5 | Fragmented; author-reputation compounding, slow | Identity dilution during transition |
| Finance essays (parallel channel) | ~3–5M | ~$8–22 | Search-driven, brutal cold start | Crowded; trust barriers |

Niche health signals (2026): gaming ≈ 40% of all YouTube watch time; raw let's-plays dead for new channels; essays that "package games rather than play them" are the breakout format; essay viewers binge start-to-finish and convert to fans of the writer. The structural wind is at this plan's back — the risk is entirely in the transition, not the destination.

**The decisive internal datapoint:** the channel's one broadening experiment (46-min OSRS-vs-WoW crossover) underperformed every pure-OSRS essay despite the largest production effort. Diagnosis per `channel-context.md`: it was framed as a comparison (homework) rather than a standalone provocation, and it was served to an OSRS-subscriber base that didn't ask for WoW. Both mistakes are correctable and both corrections are load-rated into this plan (§5, §6).

## 4. The phase plan

Each phase states its job, its content mix, its gate (the metric that opens the next phase), and its rollback. Cadence assumption throughout: **2 uploads/month** (the catalogue's demonstrated pace: 7 videos in ~5 months). If real capacity differs, stretch the calendar, never the gates.

### Phase 0 — Stabilize the base (months 0–2)
**Job:** re-prove the hit formula and bank goodwill before touching anything.
**Mix:** 4 pure-OSRS contrarian standalones from the strongest inventory (Project V #1 "92 Is Half of 99" and #4 Collection Log lead; V/N remainder behind them). No experiments. Every title a standalone provocation — the trilogy-decay lesson (50K→4.4K→2.5K) says never ship "part N" again.
**Gate to phase 1:** two consecutive uploads ≥15K views in 30 days *or* channel passes 30K subs — evidence the machine is warm.
**Rollback:** none needed; this phase is the baseline.

### Phase 1 — Register consolidation (months 2–6)
**Job:** finish converting the *voice* while the subject stays OSRS — make "sounds like this writer" the channel's most recognizable property. This phase is already half-done (the exurb1a dial, Sisyphus).
**Mix:** OSRS topics, register pushed per script's voice dial; publish **Sisyphus-OSRS as the phase capstone** with deliberately double-audience packaging — title/thumbnail legible to non-players ("A Game About Rolling a Boulder Forever"), case study OSRS. This is the first *probe* (§5) and the phase's measurement event.
**Gate to phase 2:** on the probe video, ≥25% of views from non-subscribed browse/search *and* average-view-duration within 15% of channel norm — evidence outsiders will watch the voice even when the game isn't theirs.
**Rollback:** if the probe underperforms with subscribers but holds duration with outsiders, packaging was the problem — rerun with one more probe before judging the strategy. If duration collapses for both, the register is outrunning the audience: slow the dial, stay in phase 1.

### Phase 2 — Probes (months 6–12)
**Job:** test other games without leaving home. One **probe per quarter** (2 in the phase, ~1 in 6 uploads — the base keeps 80% of the schedule).
**Probe design rules:** (a) the probe's game must be one the owner has real hours in ("favorite games" is a research advantage, and per `exurb1a.md` fake-deep is the cardinal sin); (b) the essay's *thesis* must be game-transcendent (time, death, economies, ritual) so the title works for strangers; (c) never framed as versus-OSRS (the crossover lesson); (d) Project W's WoW scripts are probe candidates only after re-titling to standalone claims — "Raiding Was a Job" passes, anything reading "WoW vs OSRS" fails.
**Gate to phase 3:** at least one probe matches the channel's median OSRS video on views *and* beats it on non-subscriber share — the external audience is now findable on demand.
**Rollback:** probes structurally underperform after 3 attempts → the pivot thesis is wrong for this channel at this size; revert to phase 1 mix, bank the register gains, revisit at 100K subs. (This outcome still leaves a healthier channel than today's.)

### Phase 3 — Widening (months 12–24)
**Job:** shift the center of mass. Move to ~50/50 OSRS/other by month 18, ~1-in-3 OSRS by month 24, letting analytics set the pace game by game. Introduce at most **one new game per quarter**; depth over coverage — the channel reviews *design questions*, not releases, so it never competes on news cadence.
**Gate (pivot declared complete):** a trailing-6-month window where non-OSRS uploads match or beat OSRS uploads on views and watch time, and returning-viewer share holds ≥ its phase-1 level — the audience has converted from game-fans to writer-fans.
**Rollback:** partial — OSRS share floats back up; the channel becomes "mostly-OSRS essays with excursions," which is a fine stable state and still exceeds today's ceiling.

### Phase 4 — Steady state (month 24+)
Rotating subjects, one flagship essay/month plus one lighter piece; OSRS remains a recurring character (it's the channel's origin myth — Geller still returns to his early wells). Annual "big swing" video (the Song-About-Rockets-shaped formal experiment) as the brand's lighthouse.

## 5. Transition mechanics (the algorithm-facing rules)

1. **Package for the stranger, write for the faithful.** Every probe/widened video's title-thumbnail must require zero knowledge of the game. The game name may appear in the title only when it *is* the draw ("Raiding Was a Job" needs "WoW" nowhere; an Elden Ring essay probably wants "Elden Ring" for search). The 5-set SEO package convention already produces this — rank the stranger-legible set first during phases 2–3.
2. **Never ship a comparison as a comparison.** Cross-game insight is the channel's engine, but it lives *inside* the essay; the packaging sells one claim about one thing. (The 8.8K crossover is the tombstone this rule is carved on.)
3. **Protect the subscriber feed.** During phase 2, publish probes in a distinct visual thumbnail style (same design system, inverted palette) so OSRS subscribers learn to recognize excursions rather than feel bait-and-switched; pin a comment owning the excursion. Churn is cheaper than resentment.
4. **Playlist architecture:** "Old School" (OSRS catalogue), "Other Worlds" (probes), "Start Here" (best-of across both). End screens route strangers to same-game videos first, voice-flagships second.
5. **Community tab as the pivot's narrator.** Poll the audience before each probe ("next excursion: X or Y?") — converts dilution risk into participation, and the poll data is free market research on which games the existing audience will follow.
6. **Shorts/clips policy:** one 60–90s cut per essay (the aphorism-shaped passage — every script's `»` lines are pre-marked for this), titled for the *idea* not the game. Shorts are the cheapest way to let the writer-brand travel outside both communities.

## 6. Metrics & decision gates (the dashboard)

Track per-video, reviewed monthly; gates in §4 reference these.

| Metric | What it answers | Healthy signal |
|---|---|---|
| Non-subscriber view share (browse+search) | Is the channel discoverable beyond the base? | Rising through phases 1–3; ≥40% on probes |
| Returning-viewer share | Is the author-brand forming? | Flat-or-rising while topics widen — *the* pivot metric |
| Avg view duration vs channel norm | Does the voice hold strangers? | Probes within 15% of OSRS norm |
| CTR by traffic source | Is packaging working per audience? | Probe CTR ≥ channel median in browse |
| Subs gained per 1K views on probes | Are excursion viewers converting to writer-fans? | ≥ OSRS videos' rate by phase 3 |
| Comment lexicon (manual, qualitative) | What are they subscribing *to*? | Rising share of comments about the writing/voice vs game-specifics |

Hard rules: no gate may be judged on fewer than 2 videos; no phase advances on a single outlier; every gate decision gets logged in this file with the numbers that made it.

## 7. Production system

The system already exists and is the channel's real moat — this plan changes its inputs, not its shape.

- **Pipeline (unchanged):** idea → engagement-mapped outline → script with delivery notation → music direction → 5-set SEO package → fact flags → §2 audit. All conventions per `CLAUDE.md`/`script-template.md`.
- **Research ledgers:** one per game, on the `research/osrs-facts.md` model, opened the moment a probe game is chosen (`wow-facts.md` exists; the pattern scales). The accuracy guardrails are non-negotiable in new games *especially* — the fastest way to die in someone else's community is a wrong number in the first video there.
- **Inventory position (a real asset):** ~21 scripts banked (Sisyphus final; Projects V, V2, N pure-OSRS; W as probe candidates) ≈ 10+ months of phase 0–2 material already written. The marginal cost of the pivot's first year is packaging and production, not writing.
- **Batching:** scripts in project-batches of five (proven rhythm); footage capture batched per game per quarter; probes scheduled mid-quarter, never two in a row.
- **Capacity guardrail:** the finance channel (§9) and this pivot must never both be in a heavy-lift phase simultaneously — the calendar in §10 interleaves them.

## 8. Monetization

Sequenced to audience size; numbers are scenario math at gaming RPMs, labeled as such, not promises.

1. **AdSense (now):** at ~$2–4 RPM, phase-0 traffic (~40–80K views/mo) ≈ $100–300/mo. This funds nothing; it's a metric, not a business.
2. **Sponsorships (from ~50K subs / consistent 30K+ views):** game-adjacent but audience-adult — VPNs, learning platforms, game stores; essay channels command above-gaming rates because of watch-through. Realistic $8–20 CPM on integrations → $250–600 per sponsored video at 30K views. Rule: one integration per two uploads maximum; the intimacy of the register is the product, and it doesn't survive wall-to-wall reads.
3. **Patreon/memberships (launch at phase 2 gate):** the author-brand model monetizes patronage unusually well — essay audiences pay for the writer (Geller-tier channels run 4-figure-plus monthly Patreons; unverified from memory, benchmark before launch). Offer: scripts as written documents (the repo's formats are literally the product), early cuts, topic polls. Target 0.5–1% of subs converting.
4. **The long tail (phase 3+):** essay back-catalogue is evergreen — design analysis doesn't patch out the way meta content does; catalogue views become the annuity. Book-shaped compilation of scripts as the eventual merch (the exurb1a playbook itself: the videos sell the writing).

Scenario table (24-month horizon, 2 uploads/mo, ranges honest):

| Scenario | Subs @ M24 | Views/mo | Ads | Sponsors | Patronage | Total/mo |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative (rollback at phase 2) | 45K | 100K | $250–400 | $300–600 | $200–500 | ~$0.8–1.5K |
| Base (gates pass on schedule) | 120K | 300K | $700–1.2K | $1.5–3K | $800–2K | ~$3–6K |
| Upside (a probe breaks out) | 300K+ | 800K+ | $2–3K | $5–10K | $3–6K | ~$10–19K |

For calibration: the same Base audience at finance RPMs would roughly triple the ads line — which is why the portfolio (§9) exists.

## 9. Portfolio: this pivot and the finance channel

They are one operation with two storefronts. Allocation by phase:

- **Phases 0–1 (months 0–6):** ~70% hours here (the base must not wobble), ~30% finance launch slate (L1–L3 + credit score, per `scripts/project-f/launch-topics.md`). The finance scripts are written; production is the only cost.
- **Phase 2 (6–12):** rebalance by data — the finance kill/commit checkpoint (any video ≥25K views or channel ≥3–5K subs by month 6) lands mid-phase. Winner gets 60%.
- **Phase 3+ (12–24):** whichever channel's marginal hour earns more keeps earning it; the plan's only dogma is that *neither* gets abandoned while passing its gates, because the two hedge different risks (game-niche ceiling vs. finance cold-start).
- **Shared assets:** register, pipeline, script conventions, the writer. Cross-pollination allowed late (a "games sell hope" / "loot-box economics" essay belongs to both channels; publish the design cut here, the money cut there — different scripts, shared research).

## 10. Risk register

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Identity dilution: algorithm mis-classifies during widening | Medium-high | Phase gates + probe quota (1-in-6) + packaging rules §5; rollback paths defined per phase |
| The 8.8K pattern repeats (base rejects excursions) | Medium | Probes are thesis-led standalones, never comparisons; distinct excursion thumbnail language; community-tab polls pre-sell each probe |
| Burnout: two channels + full pipeline | Medium-high | 21-script inventory absorbs a year; capacity guardrail §7; cadence stretches before quality does — the register cannot be rushed and survive |
| Single-writer dependency | Certain | It's the product. Mitigate mechanically: scripts banked ≥2 ahead, evergreen catalogue, no news-cycle obligations |
| exurb1a-imitation criticism as visibility grows | Medium | The influence is a register, not a costume: original theses, original research, games he'll never cover. Never deny the influence; metabolize it (every essayist has parents). If a video reads as pastiche in drafting, the §2 audit kills it |
| Favorite-game wells run dry / passion topics don't overlap audience topics | Low-medium | Community polls constrain probe selection to the intersection; the thesis-first rule means any game with one real design question qualifies |
| Platform/RPM shifts | Low | Patronage + evergreen catalogue reduce ad dependency; the finance channel is itself the hedge |

## 12. What this plan refuses to do

- No abrupt rebrand, no catalogue purge, no "new direction" announcement video. The pivot is revealed by the work, retroactively obvious.
- No release-calendar chasing (new-game launch content) — the channel's essays must be as evergreen as its back catalogue's economics require.
- No advancing a phase on vibes. The gates are the plan; a gate skipped is the plan abandoned.
- No pretending the ceiling is the point. At every gate, the honest comparison is against the *portfolio's* best marginal hour — including the one where this channel stays happily mid-sized in a niche it owns, and the finance channel becomes the growth engine. The pivot is a bet worth making precisely because it's structured to be cheap to lose.
